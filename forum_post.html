🎮 We Made a Browser Game That REQUIRES a Gamepad (And Here's Why It's Better)

TL;DR: Airlock: The Oncoming Swarm is a hardcore sci-fi survival shooter that deliberately disables keyboard controls. Before you grab your pitchforks, hear me out—this "controversial" design choice creates the most visceral space station survival experience you'll get in a browser.

Why We Ditched Keyboard Controls (And You Should Be Excited About It)

Look, I know what you're thinking. "Gamepad-only in 2026? What about accessibility?" But here's the thing—we didn't disable keyboard support to exclude players. We did it to create a superior gameplay experience.

Airlock uses one-stick combat where your left stick controls both movement AND aiming direction. No twin-stick confusion, no WASD+mouse coordination hell. Just pure, instinctual positioning and reaction time. Your character faces where you're moving, fires where you're looking, and every dodge roll feels like a natural extension of your survival instincts.

Try explaining that elegance with a keyboard. Go ahead, I'll wait. 🎮

The Situation: You're Trapped, You're Suffocating, and the Swarm is Coming

Picture this: You're fighting through 12 increasingly hellish sectors of a compromised space station. The alien infestation is spreading faster than you can clear it. Your oxygen is depleting with every breath, and once it hits zero? Contamination starts eating away at your health.

But here's where it gets fun—you have a safe room. One single sanctuary where you can breathe, rearm with randomized weapon drops, and plan your next desperate push into the swarm. No saves, no checkpoints, just one brutal gauntlet from start to finish.

The Arsenal That Keeps You Alive (Barely)

  • Shiv Pistol: Your reliable starter. Conserve that ammo.
  • Incinerator Carbine: Short-range fury that drinks fuel like crazy
  • UV Arc Cutter: Satisfying beam weapon that overheats if you get trigger-happy
  • Spore Needlegun: Late-game precision for those nightmare stalker enemies
  • Pulse Shotgun: When you absolutely need everything in front of you to stop existing

Each weapon has its own resource system (ammo, fuel, heat), so weapon choice becomes a tactical decision based on your current supplies and what hell you're about to walk into.

Technical Wizardry That'll Blow Your Mind

Here's where things get actually insane:

🎵 100% Procedural Heavy Metal Soundtrack

No audio files. None. Zero. The entire heavy metal soundtrack—distorted power chords, driving drums, atmospheric drones—is generated in real-time using Web Audio API. The music dynamically shifts from ambient tension in menus to full metal fury when the swarm hits. It's like having a live metal band that responds to your gameplay.

⚡ Console Quality in 1.4MB

This isn't some janky browser game. We're talking smooth 60fps action with particle effects, dynamic lighting, and complex enemy AI patterns—all in a package smaller than most mobile game icons. No downloads, no installs, just plug in your controller and you're in the fight.

🎯 Smart Enemy Spawning

The swarm isn't just mindless. Enemies spawn based on distance from objectives, maintain safety zones around critical areas, and use forward-spawn mechanics to keep pressure constant without feeling cheap.

This Is What Peak "Just One More Run" Feels Like

Every failed run teaches you something. Maybe you pushed too deep without checking your oxygen. Maybe you wasted precious UV Arc Cutter heat on weak enemies when you needed it for the hive nodes. Maybe you should have grabbed that Pulse Shotgun earlier.

The beauty of the gauntlet design is that every decision matters. No save scumming, no "loading last checkpoint" crutches. Just pure skill progression and game knowledge building over time.

Sector themes evolve from claustrophobic maintenance tunnels to sprawling alien-infested bio-domes. Enemy pressure escalates from manageable skirmishes to overwhelming swarm encounters. Visual complexity ramps up from sterile station corridors to organic nightmare fuel.
